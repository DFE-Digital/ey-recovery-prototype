About this training course

The course has 3 modules. Each module should take between 1 and 2 hours to complete.

You can pause and restart the training as much as you like.

Module 1: Child development and the EYFS

As an early years practitioner, understanding how children develop will enable you to meet the needs of all the children in your care. This module will help you to reflect on why knowledge of child development is important and help you to effectively implement the EYFS. It also considers the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on children.

What you’ll learn

On completion of this module you will be able to:

  • explain the importance of early childhood education and care including strategies you can use to provide a positive approach
  • explore the importance of child development knowledge including an awareness of contributing factors
  • consider effective delivery of the EYFS
  • describe the impact that the COVID-19 impact has had on childrens’ development

Module 2: Brain development and how children learn

The rapid changes that happen in the brain in a child’s first years lay critical foundations for later childhood and adult life.As an early years practitioner, you can play a significant role in encouraging and supporting brain development.This module will reinforce your understanding of how a child’s brain develops and how to consider factors affecting brain development when planning for your setting.

What you’ll learn

On completion of this module you will be able to:

  • establish and explore the stages of children’s brain development
  • explore the impact of brain development on children’s holistic development
  • examine the connection between brain development and children’s learning
  • explore a range of theories underpinning best practice

Module 3: Supporting childrens’ personal, social and emotional development

In your everyday practice you’ll be helping children to learn, develop and rehearse their key personal, social and emotional skills.This module will help you to consider how the development of those skills impact on a child’s overall development.

What you’ll learn

On completion of this module you will be able to:

  • establish and explore the stages of children’s personal, social and emotional development
  • discuss the importance of effective relationships
  • explore factors affecting behaviour
  • explain and explore the role of self-regulation
